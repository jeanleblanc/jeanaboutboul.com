## Motivation

Topology optimization seeks to find the optimal material distribution within a
design domain that minimizes a given objective (e.g., compliance) subject to
constraints on volume.

## Problem Formulation

We minimize the structural compliance:

$$
\min_{\rho} \quad C(\rho) = \mathbf{f}^T \mathbf{u}
$$

subject to the equilibrium equation $\mathbf{K}(\rho)\mathbf{u} = \mathbf{f}$
and a volume constraint $\int_\Omega \rho \, d\Omega \leq V^*$.

The density field $\rho(x) \in [0, 1]$ is penalized using the SIMP model:

$$
E(\rho) = E_{\min} + \rho^p (E_0 - E_{\min})
$$

where $p = 3$ is the penalization exponent.

## Adjoint Sensitivity

The gradient of the objective with respect to the design variables is computed
efficiently using the adjoint method, avoiding the need for $n$ forward solves:

$$
\frac{dC}{d\rho_e} = -\mathbf{u}_e^T \frac{\partial \mathbf{K}_e}{\partial \rho_e} \mathbf{u}_e
$$

## Results

The optimizer converges to a near-optimal truss-like structure in approximately
100 iterations, with the adjoint method reducing computation time by a factor
of $\sim 50\times$ compared to finite differences.
